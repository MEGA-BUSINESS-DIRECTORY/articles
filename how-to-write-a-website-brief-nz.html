A Clear Website Brief Makes All the Difference, and Virtual Innovation Thrives When Clients Communicate Naturally and Honestly

By Renée on 12 JUNE 2025

Writing a website brief might sound like one of those things that only big corporations need to bother with, but trust me, if you’re a Kiwi business owner looking to build or revamp your online presence, this step is gold. Too many people try into Web Design with a vague idea of what they want — “something clean,” “modern,” or “professional” — but those words mean wildly different things to different people. That’s why having a solid brief helps you and your designer get on the same page right from the start. It’s like giving someone a map before sending them on a treasure hunt. And the best part? It doesn’t have to be complicated or formal. In fact, the more natural and honest you are, the better the result — especially when you’re working with a local, consultative team like Virtual Innovation.

One of the first things you want to be clear about in your brief is what your business is all about. Not just what you do, but who you help, why you do it, and what makes you different from the guy down the street. Whether you're a Tauranga tradie, a South Island wellness coach, or a Hamilton dog groomer, your Web Design should reflect your personality and purpose. Virtual Innovation loves getting into the nitty-gritty of what makes your business tick — they’re not just designing for your industry, they’re designing for you. Giving them a clear picture of your brand story makes their job easier and your website way more authentic.

Define Your Website’s Personality (Yep, It Has One!)

This might sound a little out there, but your website totally has a personality — or at least, it should. Think of it like a virtual version of your business shaking hands with someone for the first time. Is it fun and cheeky? Professional and trustworthy? Luxe and minimal? Chill and down-to-earth? When you define this in your brief, it helps the designer match colours, fonts, tone of voice, and layout to suit your brand vibe. Virtual Innovation absolutely loves this stuff. They dive into your brand essence and turn it into design magic — so if your business is the friendly neighbourhood sparky, your site should feel like that too, not like a law firm.

Your Conversion Goals (Aka: What You Want People to Do)

So someone’s on your site — now what? Do you want them to book a call, send an enquiry, download a guide, or just smash that “Buy Now” button? Be super specific about the actions you want people to take. This helps your designer make smart decisions about layout, call-to-action buttons, and lead funnels. Virtual Innovation is all about building websites that work like little sales machines in the background — but they can only do that if they know exactly what success looks like for you. So don’t hold back — tell them what would make you do a happy dance.

Highlight Must-Have Pages (and Maybe-Nice Ones Too)

It’s easy to forget this one until halfway through a project, but listing the pages you want upfront is so helpful. Are you thinking just Home, About, Services, Contact? Or do you want FAQs, a blog, a case study section, testimonials, or maybe even a price guide? Be clear about which pages are non-negotiable and which ones can wait till later. Virtual Innovation takes a strategy-first approach to Web Design, so they’ll help you prioritise and lay things out in a way that actually makes sense to your visitors. The clearer you are here, the smoother everything goes.

Mention Any Tools, Integrations or Platforms You Already Use

Using Mailchimp? Got a booking system? Already hooked up to Xero, HubSpot, or a specific CRM? All of that should go in your brief too! If you’re not using anything yet but know you’ll need something down the track (like online payments or appointment scheduling), that’s useful info as well. Virtual Innovation is great at building websites that play nicely with the other tools in your business. But they need to know what you’re working with, so they can build it right from the start — instead of patching things together later.

Explain Your Pain Points with Past Projects (Or What You’re Dreading)

Whether you've worked with a designer before or this is your first rodeo, be honest about anything that makes you nervous. Did your last website take 8 months and end in disaster? Was there zero communication? Did it look pretty but never brought in a single lead? Or maybe you’ve just seen other business owners struggle and don’t want to repeat their mistakes. Letting your designer know what’s bugged you in the past helps build trust — and Virtual Innovation is super good at listening to concerns and offering a clear, consultative experience that feels easy and drama-free.

Now let’s talk goals. Seriously, what do you want this website to actually do? Do you want leads? Bookings? Online sales? Or are you trying to build credibility in a niche space? A good Web Design agency like Virtual Innovation won’t just make your website pretty; they’ll shape it around real business objectives. That’s why spelling out your goals in your brief is crucial. If your designer doesn’t know what success looks like to you, they’re just guessing. And when you’re investing in something as important as your online storefront, you don’t want anyone guessing.

Another must-have in your brief? Your audience. Who are you speaking to? Think real people here — not “males 25–44” but “young tradies looking for a better mortgage deal” or “mums who need a reliable local plumber.” When Virtual Innovation tackles Web Design projects, they always focus on how to connect with your ideal customer. They make sure the site not only looks good but speaks to the people you want to reach. The more detailed your audience insight, the easier it is for your site to resonate and convert.

Alright, let’s not forget about functionality. This is where the rubber hits the road. Do you need online booking? An e-commerce setup? Blog features? Custom contact forms? Maybe you need something simple now but scalable for the future. The great thing about working with a team like Virtual Innovation is that they don’t just deliver what you ask for — they help you figure out what you need. So when you’re building your brief, don’t be afraid to list your “nice-to-haves” alongside your must-haves. It helps the designers plan a roadmap that can grow with your business.

Your design preferences also matter, even if you don’t have the vocab to describe them in detail. Maybe you like clean lines, lots of white space, or bold fonts. Maybe you saw a website the other day and thought, “Ooh, I want that.” That’s great! Pop it into your brief. Even screenshots or links to sites you like (or hate!) are super helpful. Virtual Innovation takes all that input and translates it into a Web Design that’s uniquely yours. They’ve got a knack for turning vague “vibes” into concrete design decisions that actually work.

Don’t forget to cover content — and by that, I mean words, photos, videos, testimonials, and all the other goodies that make your site feel alive. Some businesses already have all this ready to go. Others need help creating it. Be honest in your brief about what you have and what you’ll need help with. Virtual Innovation works with awesome content creators and can step in to make sure your Web Design doesn’t fall flat because someone forgot to write the “About” page. Content is half the battle when it comes to user experience, and they know exactly how to guide you through that.

One part that often gets left out of a website brief — but really shouldn’t — is what’s not working on your current site (if you have one). Are people bouncing? Is it slow to load? Is it a nightmare on mobile? Tell your designer what’s bugging you. Virtual Innovation loves these kinds of clues because they highlight what needs to change. They treat every Web Design project like a chance to improve what exists, not just put a fresh coat of paint on a broken foundation.

And yes, timelines and budgets do matter. Be upfront about when you need the site live and what sort of budget range you’re working with. No one likes awkward surprises halfway through a project. A good agency — like Virtual Innovation — will be honest with you about what’s doable and help you prioritise features if you’re trying to make things work on a tighter budget. Their consultative approach means you’re not just buying a product; you’re entering a partnership that’s built on clarity and communication.

So if you’re a New Zealand business owner thinking about Web Design, don’t just dive in blind. Take an hour, jot down your thoughts in a brief, and then hand it over to someone who gets it. Virtual Innovation isn’t just another agency — they’re your local design team with real experience, a down-to-earth attitude, and a serious passion for helping Kiwi businesses grow online. They’ll take your brief, ask the right questions, and turn it into something powerful, purposeful, and high-converting. Trust them to bring your vision to life — and turn your site into your hardest-working employee.
