Rough Or Cracked Pool Surfaces Mean It’s Time To Resurface, And Dependable Pool Builders Will Help You Know Exactly When To Act

By Renée on 25 JUNE 2025

You know that feeling when you’re walking barefoot into your pool and it feels more like stepping onto a sidewalk than gliding into cool, smooth water? That’s one of the first signs your pool is trying to tell you something. It’s not being rude, promise—just letting you know it might be time to resurface. Many homeowners don’t even realize their pool needs attention until things feel… off. That gritty, sandpaper-like texture underfoot? Yeah, it’s not supposed to be there. A good pool surface should feel pleasant, not like it’s exfoliating your toes. That’s where calling a reliable pool builder near me comes into play—they’ll help you figure out what’s normal and what’s overdue for a makeover.

Another thing to keep an eye out for? Stains. And no, not the kind you can scrub off with a little elbow grease and a stiff brush. These are those stubborn, deep-set stains that laugh at your pool cleaner. They can be caused by algae, minerals, or even just years of water chemistry doing its thing. When your pool starts looking like a tie-dye project gone wrong, that’s a huge clue it’s time to resurface. You want that vibrant, inviting blue again—not fifty shades of blotchy. A professional pool builder near me can come in, assess the surface, and recommend the best material to bring your pool back to its prime.

Understanding Different Pool Surfaces and Their Lifespan

Not all pool surfaces are created equal, and knowing the type of finish you have can really help you gauge when resurfacing might be due. Plaster, pebble, quartz, and tile all age differently, and some last longer than others. If you’ve got plaster, for instance, it usually needs resurfacing every 7 to 15 years, depending on use and maintenance. So, if you’re googling “pool builder near me” and wondering why your plaster looks tired, now you know why. A pool builder near me can help identify your surface type and suggest the perfect time to refresh it.

How Weather and Climate Can Affect Your Pool’s Surface

Living in Florida means your pool gets a lot of sun and rain—both of which can take a toll on your pool’s surface. Constant exposure to UV rays can fade colors and dry out materials, while heavy rain and humidity can promote algae growth and damage finishes over time. If your pool’s been through a few tough seasons, that’s totally normal, but it also means you might need resurfacing sooner than you think. A trusted pool builder near me knows exactly how to protect and restore your pool against Florida’s unique climate challenges.

The Importance of Regular Maintenance to Extend Surface Life

Believe it or not, a little TLC can go a long way in keeping your pool surface in good shape. Regular cleaning, balancing water chemistry, and avoiding harsh chemicals help prevent stains, scaling, and rough spots. If you’ve been skipping maintenance, your pool might be aging faster than expected. But don’t worry—that’s another great reason to chat with a pool builder near me. They can help you create a maintenance plan to extend your pool’s life and pinpoint when resurfacing becomes a smart next step.

How Resurfacing Can Improve Pool Safety

Safety might not be the first thing on your mind when you’re thinking about resurfacing, but it should be! Rough or cracked surfaces can be uncomfortable or even dangerous for kids and adults alike. Plus, uneven or sharp edges increase the risk of scrapes and slips. When the surface is smooth, non-porous, and freshly finished, your pool becomes a much safer spot for everyone to enjoy. Let’s talk cracks for a second. Nobody wants them—especially not in a structure that’s supposed to hold thousands of gallons of water. If you see spider web cracks forming in the plaster, don’t ignore them. Even tiny ones can be signs of bigger structural issues brewing beneath the surface. They may start out harmless but can expand with time, heat, and shifting ground. These cracks are your pool’s version of a frown—it’s asking for help. A local pool builder near me can check for damage, seal it properly, and give your pool a fresh new coat that looks amazing and holds strong.

Then there’s the not-so-fun mystery of vanishing water. Ever notice your pool seems to need constant topping off? Sure, some evaporation is normal, especially in hot Florida weather, but if you're filling it up more often than you're swimming in it, that’s a problem. Water loss might mean you’ve got leaks, and one common culprit? Deteriorating plaster or broken seals. Instead of just topping it off endlessly (and racking up that water bill), it might be smarter to have a pool builder near me check for signs that resurfacing could fix those leaks once and for all.

Over time, sun exposure, chemicals, and use take a toll. The finish gets dull, blotchy, and loses that beautiful shimmer it once had. If your pool has started to look a little tired—even if it’s still technically working—it might be time to resurface for cosmetic and functional reasons.
