The Best Time To Book Your Newborn Photoshoot Is During Those First Two Weeks When They’re Tiny, Sleepy, And Perfect For Capturing Those Curled-Up Poses

By Renne on 29 December 2024

Booking a newborn photoshoot is something you’ll cherish for years to come, but when is the best time to plan for it? Well, it turns out that timing is everything when it comes to capturing those sweet, sleepy newborn moments. Ideally, you’ll want to book your session within the first two weeks of your baby’s life. During this time, your newborn is still in that sweet, sleepy stage, which makes it easier to capture those adorable, curled-up poses. They’re also still super flexible, which allows for all those precious poses where they look like tiny, peaceful little angels. Trust me, it’s hard to get those dreamy, curled-up shots after that initial window, so the sooner you book, the better!

Now, I know that after you’ve just had a baby, the last thing you’re probably thinking about is scheduling appointments. But here’s the good news — Elena Mason Photography makes the process as easy and stress-free as possible. In fact, most parents book their session while they’re still pregnant, typically around the 20-30 week mark. That way, you don’t have to worry about scrambling for availability once your little one arrives. Elena’s calendar fills up quickly, especially during peak seasons, so booking in advance ensures you get the date and time that works best for you.

1. Consider Your Newborn’s Comfort and Timing

When booking your newborn photoshoot, one important thing to keep in mind is your baby’s comfort. Newborns are still adjusting to life outside the womb, and they need plenty of time for feeding, changing, and naps. It’s a good idea to book your photoshoot at a time when your baby is usually calm and relaxed. If your little one tends to be most content in the mornings, try to schedule the shoot during that time. Elena Mason Photography takes this into account, working around your baby’s schedule so the shoot can be a peaceful, enjoyable experience for everyone involved. The less stressed you and your baby are, the better the photos will turn out!

2. Book Early for Special Props or Themes

If you have a specific theme or props in mind for your newborn photoshoot, it’s a good idea to book early so you can discuss these details with Elena ahead of time. Whether you want a rustic, vintage theme with delicate blankets and wooden baskets, or something a little more playful with stuffed animals and fun outfits, the sooner you book, the better. Elena can source unique props that align with your vision, and she’s always ready to help guide you in picking out the perfect items that will make your photos even more special. Booking early gives you more time to collaborate and ensures your ideas come to life.

3. Factor in Time for Post-Processing and Prints

After your newborn photoshoot, there’s another important thing to think about—post-processing! Elena takes great care in editing each photo to make sure it’s perfect, so this can take some time. You’ll want to leave a little breathing room between your photoshoot and any special events or announcements, just in case. If you’re planning to use the photos for birth announcements or a special family gift, booking your session early gives Elena enough time to work her magic. Once the photos are ready, you’ll have the option to choose prints, albums, or digital files. The extra time also allows for a more thoughtful selection process as you decide which images to cherish forever.

4. Know That Every Baby Is Unique—And So Is Every Photoshoot

While the general timing guidelines for newborn photoshoots are a great starting point, every baby is different. Some babies might sleep soundly through most of the session, while others may want to stay awake and engage with the photographer. Either way, it’s important to approach your newborn photoshoot with flexibility in mind. Elena Mason Photography is great at adapting to your baby’s unique personality, capturing all their precious little quirks, whether they’re snoozing peacefully or wide-eyed and curious. Remember that there’s no “one-size-fits-all” approach to newborn photography—Elena works with you and your baby’s needs to get the best shots.

5. Schedule a Pre-Shoot Consultation to Discuss Your Vision

Another great way to plan for the best newborn photoshoot experience is to schedule a pre-shoot consultation with Elena. This gives you a chance to chat about what you envision for the session—whether you want simple, minimalist photos or something more elaborate with fun props and family participation. You can also go over practical details, like any special requests or sensitivities your baby might have. Elena’s goal is to make sure you feel comfortable and confident leading up to the session, and this consultation is the perfect way to make sure everything is in place before the big day. Plus, it gives you a chance to meet Elena, see her studio, and get a feel for the overall vibe of the shoot.

Booking ahead doesn’t just secure your spot on Elena’s calendar; it also gives you plenty of time to think about the little details of the shoot. You’ll have time to decide on the perfect outfits, discuss any special props or themes you’d like to incorporate, and think about any personal touches that will make the session even more meaningful. Elena is fantastic at guiding you through all these decisions, whether you’re unsure about what to wear or need advice on setting up the most beautiful shots for your baby. She’ll take care of the technical details, from lighting to positioning, so all you need to do is show up and enjoy the experience.
