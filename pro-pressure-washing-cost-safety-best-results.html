2. The Risk of Property Damage

DIY pressure washing sounds like a simple task, but it can actually be quite risky if you don’t know what you’re doing. The high pressure from a pressure washer is no joke—it can damage your siding, windows, roof, and even your lawn. For instance, using too much pressure on delicate surfaces like wood siding or shingles can strip paint, break seals, and cause water to seep into places it shouldn’t. If you accidentally aim the nozzle in the wrong direction, you might even crack your driveway or patio, which would end up costing you much more in repairs than what you would’ve paid for professional service.
